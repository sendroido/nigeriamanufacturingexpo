Conference

Learn from industry leaders sharing their knowledge and experience. Attendance is free of charge for all visitors.
The free-to-attend conference at the Nigerian 2020 aims to create a platform where industry experts can share their knowledge, expertise and experience.

Why attend?

  • Hear from industry leaders sharing knowledge and best practice
  • Learn capacity building and business management training - up skill your team
  • Gain insights from real-life business case studies
  • Get Finance and Funding Advice from the experts

Highlight

The current and developing environment in which disruptive technologies and trends such as Block chain; Internet of Things; Machine Learning; Artificial Intelligence; Driverless car and Augmented Realty, among others, would dictate the pace of development among nations. Therefore, the need to start using technology to produce our own food, products and other indigenous innovations becomes imperative so that we do not end dependent on developed countries.

Key Discussion Points

  • Role of research & development in driving systemic change for industrial renaissance.
  • Improving our educational curricula for greater participation in Science & Technology?
  • Information technology as a driving force for sustainable industrial development
  • Developmental Industrial changes as a sign of transformation using Internet of Things (IoT)
  • The wave of technological advancement in raw materials development, production, management and governance
  • Availability of local raw materials; The raw material supply chain, linking the cart & the horse; the journey so far in locating the raw materials by the manufacturers.
  • Using disruptive technology in boosting production through enhancement of local raw material.
  • Indigenous Human Capital Development: Are we ready for the 5th Industrial Revolution
  • Investment opportunities & Foreign Participation in Raw Materials & Manufacturing Development
  • Future Trends & Emerging Technologies in Manufacturing – Advanced Manufacturing: Where are we
